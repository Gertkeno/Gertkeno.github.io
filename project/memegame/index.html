Legend

Stupid Concepts, 100% Effort

From the start of my SDL2 career I've started projects themed around things I find silly, like both the overtly edgy games I made killing minions or a wave-based dad shooter. I used these themes to build tests I would be satisfied with, rather than trying to make some sort of magnum opus while learning SDL2's features or a new C++ concept. I do genuinely like what I've made from a programming perspective, but I can't say I'm proud of these projects thematically.

When I attempted a serious project title to be determined, I was, and still am re-writing so much code due to style or fundamentals being wrong it's tough to keep going. Where in a test/stupid project I'd just drop it at that point, take what I learned and attempt to apply it better next time. Making it a miracle I uploaded four games.

Anyways on to Meme Game specifically.


The Plan And Why

I wanted to make a boss fight centered game, it seemed like a good genre to code a lot of specific, odd, and unique actions. I knew from my previous project, title to be determined, making art for games sucks. I wanted to do 90% code, 10% art. Doing so little of my own art would be tough for an original and creative project, at least without hiring a dedicated artist.

Luckily I'm part of a community full of artists that to some degree appreciate specific memes, instead of paying them what little money I don't have, I could just pull stuff from the internet. By pandering to this community I'm almost guaranteed some kind of audience, thus two problems solved in one decision. So now we have a genre I really enjoy, the best possible theme for a silly game, and an audience I love.

I start making the game with Asteroids/LUFTRAUSERS movement and a basic hit/hurt circle system. Simple sine of angle and radius checks, tried and true stuff.
